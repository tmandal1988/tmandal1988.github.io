Currently, I am a Flight Controls and Simulation Engineer for project Vahana in A3 by Airbus at Santa Clara, California, USA. As a part of the team I am involved in design, analysis, implementation and flight testing of autonomous full-scale eletric VTOL tilt wing aircraft. I obtained my Ph.D in Aerospace Engineering from West Vriginia University (WVU), USA in 2016. While at WVU I worked in the Interactive Robotics Laboratory (IRL) as a graduate research assistant for Dr. Yu Gu.


Before joining WVU I finished my Dual Degree (B.Tech and M.Tech) in Aerospace Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, INDIA. I enjoy working on difficult problems and my research interests include autonomous robotics, human-robot interaction, guidance, controls, navigation, sensor fusion, state estimation, and machine learning. I want to continue researching ways to make intelligent robots perform complex tasks in uncertain and dynamic environments.


My Ph.D. work involved mathematically modeling the human pilot and estimating human pilot model parameters in real time to predict closed loop system instability. The research encouraged me to seek in-depth knowldege of control theory, estimation techniques (Unscented Kalman Filter and Particle Filter), optimization techniques and evolution of learning in humans. As a part of my research I led a group of three undergraduate students and one master's student to develop and flight test Pilot Research Aircraft (PIRAT) in IRL. Humans are incredible controllers, planners and decision makers and knowledge drived from such research can help in developing better robots and improve human robot interaction.


When I am not busy learning or working, I enjoy hiking with my dog, solving Rubik's cube, cooking and tinkering with machines. I am also learning to fly Cessna 172 to get my SEL PPL.
