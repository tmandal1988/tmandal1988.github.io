Currently, I am a Ph.D. candidate in Aerospace Engineering at West Vriginia University (WVU), USA where I work in the Interactive Robotics Laboratory (IRL) with Dr. Yu Gu. Before joining WVU I finished my Dual Degree (B.Tech and M.Tech) in Aerospace Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, INDIA. I enjoy working on difficult problems and my research interests include autonomous robotics, human-robot interaction, guidance, control, navigation, sensor fusion, state estimation, and machine learning. Following my graduation in August of 2016, I want to continue researching ways to make intelligent robots perform complex tasks in uncertain and dynamic environments. I hope to find internship opportunities between May-July 2016 and job opportunities in robotics beginning in August 2016.


My Ph.D. work primarily involves mathematically modeling the human pilot and estimating human pilot model parameters in real time to predict closed loop system instability. The research requires in-depth knowldege of control theory, estimation techniques (Unscented Kalman Filter and Particle Filter), optimization techniques and a general overview of chaos theory, information theory, human physiology, and evolution of learning in humans. As a part of my research I lead a group of three undergraduate students and one master's student for developing and flight testing Pilot Research Aircraft (PIRAT) in IRL. Humans are incredible controllers, planners and decision makers and knowledge drived from such research can help in developing better robots and improve human robot interaction.


When I am not busy doing research, I like hiking with my dog, solving Rubik's cubes, and tinkering and building robots. I am also learning to fly remote controlled aircrafts, and consequently then repairing them afterwards.
